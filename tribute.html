Abdul kalam

Introduction

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam attended the Madras Institute of Technology, where he received a degree in aeronautical engineering in 1960.

Early Life

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He hailed from a humble background and his father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife.

Education and Career

Abdul Kalam excelled in academics and earned degrees in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology. He began his career as a scientist at the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs, including the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

Awards and Legacy

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. Despite his demise, his legacy lives on, as he remains an iconic figure admired for his humility, intellect, and commitment to nation-building.

Presidency

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. His presidency was marked by his vision for a developed India, which he often articulated as the pursuit of "Pura (complete) and Shresth (excellent) Bharat (India)." He was widely respected for his simplicity, integrity, and dedication to the welfare of the nation.
